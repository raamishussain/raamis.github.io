About Me

I'm Raamis Hussain. I recently received my PhD in physics from the University of Wisconsin - Madison. During my PhD, I worked as a member of the IceCube Neutrino Observatory where I performed searches for high-energy neutrino emission from compact binary mergers such as binary black hole mergers. My work mainly consisted of statistical inference, data analysis, and data visualization with Python. I also spent time teaching and tutoring undergraduate students in various physics courses.

Before my PhD, I received my Bachelors of Science in physics from the University of California, Santa Barbara. I graduated with highest honors in the major and with a Research Distinction award for my work at CERN.

In my free time I enjoy cooking various types of spicy foods and spending time on the mats at my local brazilian jiu jitsu gym.
